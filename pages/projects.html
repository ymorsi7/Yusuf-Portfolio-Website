    Arduino, Python, OpenCV: Swaying Attention: Drowsiness Detection (Winter 2021)

    For our final project in ECE 16 (Rapid Software and Hardware Design) at UCSD, my partner and I used some of the skills we learning throughout the quarter to create a design that we had in mind. Swaying Attention is a project that aims to tackle the problem of students not paying attention. Students not paying attention in class was a problem when school is in person but now that most students are doing school online as a result of quarantine the problem has gotten a lot worse. Many students, including ourselves, have a hard time paying attention in online lectures and end up missing a major portion of the content. To solve this problem we created Swaying attention, a program that uses eye detection to track students dozing off and gives them a reminder to stay focused. To achieve this we used Python, OpenCV, D-lib, Arduino-C code and a Sparkfun ESP32 board. Our prototype is able to detect drowsiness and outputs a message to the student on a display as well as making noise with a buzzer to get their attention.

    Arduino, Eagle, Onshape, and Fritzing: Non-Contact Range Detection of Ultrasonic Sensor (Fall 2020)

    While taking ECE 5 (Introduction to Electrical Engineering) at UCSD, myself and two other people formed a group to create a lab assignment. For this lab, the student is meant to work with a HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor to write Arduino-C code that will print this general distance the pulse travelled upon meeting an obstacle. In addition, they need to graph it onto Arduino IDE’s serial monitor and continue to build upon the HC-SR04 by integrating three LEDs and a buzzer to signify danger zones. An IR Remote and Receiver were also added as additional components to pause the HC-SR04 sensor. If the danger zone is activated, it can be detected whether the obstacle is an object or a human being through a DHT11 temperature/humidity sensor. This will all compose into our final design: the Ultrasonic Sensor Security System.
